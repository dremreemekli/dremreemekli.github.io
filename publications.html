  • Complicated Acute Appendicitis Presenting as a Necrotizing Fasciitis of the Abdominal Wall: A Case Report

    Betül Tiryaki Baştuğ, Berkay Subaşı, Emre Emekli, Kübra Parpucu, Mahmut Kebapçı

    This case report describes a rare complication of acute appendicitis, where it progressed to necrotizing fasciitis of the abdominal wall. A 50-year-old male presented with a week-long history of severe right-sided abdominal pain. The patient’s condition rapidly worsened, showing signs of leukocytosis and acute kidney injury. CT scans revealed fluid and gas collections in the abdominal wall and inflammation around the right colon. An emergency laparotomy discovered that the appendicitis had perforated, leading to a necrotizing soft tissue infection. This case underscores the importance of early recognition and prompt surgical intervention in managing this rare but life-threatening complication.
